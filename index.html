College Radio for Beginners

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

How to make an awesome radio show to get your radio career going!

Assembly time: 4 hours
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Have you ever heard an anouncer talking about music on the radio and thought "I wish that could be me!"? Let this be your ultimate guide to following those dreams and becoming a radio dj!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Time
  • Patience
  • A recording studio
  • A Computer
  • Editing software
  • A microphone
  • Headphones

  1. Start out with an idea!

    Pro Tip: It helps to write a script so that you have something to follow

    For instance, what kind of music are you interested in? Some examples include:

    • Indie
    • Rock
    • K-pop
    • Ambient noise music

  2. Find a Place to Record

    Pro Tip: Things are bound to go a little awry. Book more time thank you think you will need.

    Check your college campus for recoridng studios! Often times journalism schools will have podcasting studios. Make sure you carve out some time to sit down and record.

    man smiles at camera in a recording studio

  3. Write a script for your show!

    Pro Tip: Twitter, Google, and Spotify are always a good resource when looking for information about artists or bands

    Open a Google Doc or a Word Doc and plan out the flow of your show.

  4. Make a hole through the cork so that the bicycle pump can easily fit into it.

    Fix the pump tube to it with plasticine.

  5. Invert the bottle and cut three flippers of cardboard.

    This is necessary to hold the rocket at launch time. Make your best design.
